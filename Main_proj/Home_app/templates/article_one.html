A person died in Egypt as a result of receiving Hitler's injection

Publication date: March 2, 2024 | 1 minute read

A

person died in Egypt as a result of receiving what is known as the Hitler injection
an injection used to quickly treat colds and influenza.
his injection is widely used without a prescription in many areas of Egypt, especially

remote villages, and it gives a quick feeling of improvement, but most people do not know its risks. This injection consists of an antibiotic, cortisone, and vitamins, a formula that is not scientifically based and is not licensed to treat the common cold.

caused by bacteria or fungi. In addition, using antibiotics without a doctor's supervision can cause anaphylactic shock, which can lead to death if not treated quickly.

Read also Colds and antibiotics

Cortisone is also not used to treat cases of influenza and colds. Rather, it is used to relieve severe swelling and pain in cases of acute pneumonia, and its use is prohibited without medical supervision due to its many side effects.

The Egyptian Ministry of Health has warned citizens against taking Hitler's injection under any circumstances, as this combination may cause a sharp drop in blood pressure, leading to cardiac arrest. The Ministry also warned against taking any injections or medications directly from pharmacies without consulting a specialist doctor, because treatment depends primarily on the correct diagnosis.
