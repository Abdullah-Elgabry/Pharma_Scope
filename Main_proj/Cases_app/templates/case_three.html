History of Present Illness

Sierra C. is a 35-year-old White woman who has experienced paresthesia of her left arm twice in the past 6 months. She did not seek treatment for the first episode, which resolved after 24 hours. The second episode, which occurred 2 weeks ago, was similar to the first episode and resolved after about 48 hours, prompting her to schedule a visit to her PCP. Evaluation by the patient's PCP was unremarkable except for mild weakness in her left hand and arm. Laboratory studies (CBC, CMP, thyroid panel, A1c, vitamin B12 level) were all within normal limits. She denied any other symptoms but reported feeling fatigued recently, which she attributed to stress related to work and worrying about her arm symptoms. She has no travel history, resides in an urban area, and denies any recent tick bites. She has no significant past medical history and takes no medications. She has an IUD for long-term birth control.

Family and Social History

Sierra has no family history of autoimmune or neurologic diseases. She is a graphic designer. She is married with no children. She and her husband do not plan to have children in the near future. She has no smoking history. She consumes about one alcoholic drink every few weeks and denies any illicit or recreational drug use.
