History of Present Illness

Shannon C. is a 22-year-old White woman presenting for a follow-up of recently diagnosed plaque psoriasis. She was initially diagnosed with mild plaque psoriasis during an urgent care visit 8 weeks ago when she presented with a painful red pruritic rash on her elbows. She was prescribed a moderate-potency topical corticosteroid (triamcinolone 0.1%) along with an emollient cream and was referred to dermatology for a follow-up. Four weeks later, she presented to dermatology and reported no relief of her symptoms despite topical treatment. BSA measurement revealed worsening of disease severity and her diagnosis was upgraded to moderate plaque psoriasis, and she was prescribed a high-potency topical corticosteroid (clobetasol 0.05%). Shannon presents today after 4 weeks of treatment with a high-potency topical corticosteroid and says her rash has spread to her lower back with increased pruritus causing her lesions to bleed. She also complains of sleep disturbance and difficulty focusing at work owing to her worsening symptoms. She has no comorbidities, has no history of allergies, and has an IUD in place for long-term contraception.

Family and Social History

Shannon C. has no family history of psoriasis, atopic dermatitis, or any other dermatologic or autoimmune disorders. She is single and is actively dating.
She works as an intern at an interior design firm. She has never smoked, drinks one to two alcoholic beverages per week, and does not use recreational or illicit drugs.
